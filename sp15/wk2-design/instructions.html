For this hands-on, you will be ideating and prototyping a website's landing page in groups of 3-4 people.
Assignment

It is an important skill for web designers to learn how to ideate, prototype, and analyze websites. For this hands-on, we will be taking prototyping up the landing page of a dating website.

Step 1: Ideation

Ideation is an important part of the design cycle which involves answering a few fundamental questions about a particular website. Take some time to think about what goes into a dating website, and see if you can answer the following questions:

  1. What is the purpose of this website?
  2. Who are the target users for this website?
  3. What are the website's most important features? (Order them by priority with respect to the given website)

Step 2: Prototype

Prototyping your user interface involves creating sketches. In your group, discuss the aforementioned questions, and proceed to sketch out what you think the website should look like on the wireframe sheets we provided you with.

Step 3: Evaluation & Presentation

The final step of the design cycle is to get feedback from your peers. Please submit your sketches to a TA. The TA's will select a few groups to present. We ask of the rest of the students to provide feedback by commenting on some of the design elements we discussed in class today including the positioning, spacing and typography used in each group's sketch.

Case Study Example

Finally, let's look at some example dating websites and see what the professionals have done. What do you think works better and what features can be improved upon given what you discussed and prototyped in your groups?
